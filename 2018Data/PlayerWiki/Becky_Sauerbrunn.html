Rebecca Elizabeth Sauerbrunn (born June 6, 1985) is an American soccer player, Olympic gold medalist and FIFA Women's World Cup champion. A veteran defender for the United States women's national soccer team, she also plays for Utah Royals FC in the National Women's Soccer League, the highest division of women's soccer in the United States. She first appeared for the United States national team at the 2008 Four Nations Tournament during a match against Canada on January 16. She has since made 155 total appearances for the team.

Sauerbrunn has represented the United States at two FIFA Women's World Cups: 2011 in Germany and 2015 in Canada. She made one appearance during the 2011 FIFA Women's World Cup, playing during the semifinal match, a 3â1 win against France. She was a starting player for the United States at the 2015 FIFA Women's World Cup, playing every minute of all seven matches for the team. She has also represented the United States at the 2012 Summer Olympics in London and made three appearances during the tournament, including the semifinal and gold medal matches. On July 14, 2016, she was named to the team that would represent the United States at the 2016 Summer Olympics in Rio de Janeiro.

Before joining FC Kansas City in 2013, she played professional soccer for the Washington Freedom, RÃ¸a IL, magicJack, and D.C. United Women. While with FC Kansas City, she was won two NWSL Championships and has been awarded NWSL Defender of the Year every year since the league's development. Before playing professional soccer, she played collegiate soccer for the University of Virginia Cavaliers and played every game in her four seasons with the team.

As of 2018, Sauerbrunn is captain of Utah Royals FC. From 2016 to 2018, she also co-captained the United States women's national team along with Carli Lloyd.[1][2]

Early life[edit]

Born to Jane and Scott Sauerbrunn in St. Louis, Missouri, Becky was raised with two older brothers, Grant and Adam.[3] Growing up, her brothers motivated her to step up for herself and become physically and mentally tough.[4]

Sauerbrunn played with the JB Marine Soccer Club beginning at age 12.[5] She helped the team win the Missouri State Cup four times as well as a Midwest Regional Championship in 2000.[3][4]

Sauerbrunn attended Ladue High School, where she was a four-year letter winner in soccer, volleyball and basketball.[citation needed] She was named soccer team captain playing as a sweeper and central midfielder. During her senior season, she scored 21 goals and made 19 assists. She was named 2003 Missouri Gatorade Player of the Year, 2002 and 2003 Parade All-American, 2001 NSCAA Youth All-American, and 2002 NSCAA Adidas (U-17) All-American.[3][5]

Sauerbrunn attended the University of Virginia and was a member of the Virginia Cavaliers women's soccer team from 2003 to 2007.

Virginia won the match in penalty kicks and advanced to the second round. In the second round, Virginia faced Villanova on November 16 and lost the match in penalty kicks, halting their advancement in the tournament.[7] Following the 2003 season, Sauerbrunn received NSCAA All-Region honors, NSCAA All-American First Team honors, All-ACC Second Team honors, and ACC All-Freshman Team honors.[8][9] She was also named ACC Freshman of the Year for 2003.[9]

Sauerbrunn missed the entire 2004 season due to national team obligations at the 2004 FIFA U-20 Women's World Championships in Thailand.[3]

In January 2013, Sauerbrunn was called up to a 29-player training camp leading up to two matches against Scotland in early February.[164] On February 21, Saeurbrunn was named to the 23-player roster for the 2013 Algarve Cup in Portugal that took place from March 6 to 13.[165] Although she did not appear in the team's first match against Iceland on March 6,[166] Sauerbrunn wore the captain's armband in her first appearance of the tournament on March 8 in a match against China.[167] She also started in a match against Sweden on March 11 and came in during the 68th minute of the final match to help the United States win the Algarve Cup title.[168][169]

Following the 2013 Algarve Cup, Sauerbrunn traveled to Europe with the national team for matches against Germany and the Netherlands in early April.[170] She appeared during the match against the Netherlands on April 9, a 3â1 win for the United States.[171] In late May, Sauerbrunn was named to the 21-player roster that traveled to Canada to train in preparation for a match against Canada on June 2.[172] She did not appear in the match.[173]

In 2014, Sauerbrunn made 22 appearances, starting 20, and played a total of 1,757 minutes.[4] She started off the year at a national team training camp from January 8 to 15 at U.S. Soccer's National Training Center in Carson, California.[174] Following the training camp, Sauerbrunn made her 50th appearance for the national team in a match against Canada on January 31 in Frisco, Texas. She was the 46th player in United States women's national team history to reach 50 caps.[175]

On February 24, Sauerbrunn was named to the 24-player roster for the 2014 Algarve Cup that took place from March 5 to 12.[176] She wore the captain's armband during the team's first match of the tournament on March 5 against Japan, a 1â1 draw.[177] She also started in the match against Sweden on March 7, a game that ended the 43-game unbeaten streak for the United States.[178] Sauerbrunn came in during the 62nd minute of the match against Denmark on March 10, a 5â3 defeat for the United States.[179] Sauerbrunn started in the match against Korea DPR that secured their seventh place finish in the tournament.[180]

Sauerbrunn joined the team for a two-game series against China in April. She started in both matches and wore the captain's armband in the second game on April 10.[181][182] In late April, Sauerbrunn was named to a 22-player roster for a match against Canada on May 8.[183] She played all 90 minutes in the match, which ended in a 1â1 draw.[184] She was then named to the roster for two games against France on June 14 and 19.[185] She started both matches.[186][187] Sauerbrunn was named to a 19-player roster for a match against Switzerland on August 20 in Sandy, Utah.[188] She started the match and was replaced by Julie Johnston in the second half. Following the CONCACAF Qualifying tournament, Sauerbrunn was named to the 24-player roster for the International Tournament of Brasilia in Brazil that took place from December 10 to 21.[197] Sauerbrunn started all four games of the tournament. 2015 FIFA Women's World Cup[edit]

Sauerbrunn in 2015

Sauerbrunn started all 25 matches for the United States in 2015, the only player to do so. She also had the most minutes played on the team with 2,184.[4]

Sauerbrunn started off the year at a 21-day training camp in 2015 from January 5 to 25 at the U.S. Soccer National Training Center in Carson, California.[200] Following the training camp, Sauerbrunn was named to the 24-player team that would travel on a 13-day trip to Europe for matches against France and England in mid-February.[201] Sauerbrunn played all 90 minutes in the match against France on February 8. She made a key save in the opening minutes of the game and assisted in halting the opposing team's attack. She was subsequently named Budweiser Woman of the Match.[202] She also played all 90 minutes in the match against England on February 13, which was a shutout victory for the United States.[203]

On February 21, Sauerbrunn was named to the 25-player roster for the 2015 Algarve Cup in Portugal.[204] She started all four of the matches for the United States and was named Budweiser Woman of the Match for the 0â0 draw against Iceland on March 9.[4][205] She was then named to a 25-player roster on March 20 for a match against New Zealand on April 4 in St. Louis.[206] She started in the match and played all 90 minutes, helping the United States defeat New Zealand 4â0.[207]

On April 14, 2015, Sauerbrunn was named to the 23-player roster that would represent the United States at the 2015 FIFA Women's World Cup.[208] She played every minute in all seven matches for the United States during the World Cup.[4] Sauerbrunn became a World Cup Champion on July 5, when she helped the United States defeat Japan 5â2 in the Women's World Cup final.[209] Sauerbrunn joined the national team on a Victory Tour following their World Cup win that started in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania on August 16 and ended in New Orleans, Louisiana on December 16.[210][211]

2016 Summer Olympics[edit]

On January 9, 2016, Sauerbrunn was named captain of the United States women's national team along with Carli Lloyd.[1]

Sauerbrunn joined the national team for their first training camp of the year at the U.S. Soccer National Training Center in Carson, California from January 5 to 21.[212] She was then named to the 20-player roster for 2016 CONCACAF Women's Olympic Qualifying.[213] The United States qualified to the 2016 Olympic Games in Rio de Janeiro after a semifinal win against Trinidad & Tobago on February 19.[214] Sauerbrunn made her 100th appearance for the national team on February 21 in the final against Canada. During the match, she also made her third career assist. The United States won the tournament after defeating Canada 2â0. Following the tournament, Sauerbrunn was named to the 2016 CONCACAF Women's Olympic Qualifying Best XI.[215]

Sauerbrunn was named to the roster for the 2016 SheBelieves Cup that took place from March 3 to 9.[216] She started in the team's opening match of the tournament on March 3 against England. Sauerbrunn was noted for her good positioning during the match, helping block a shot in the 59th minute.[217] She also made appearances in other two matches of the tournament, helping the United States win the 2016 SheBelieves Cup with a 2â1 win over Germany in their final game.[218][219]

Sauerbrunn joined a 23-player roster for a training camp ahead of two matches against Colombia in early April.[220] She appeared in both matches, but did not play all 90 minutes in either game.[221][222] Sauerbrunn was on the roster for another two-game series against Japan in early June[223] and she started in both games.[224][225]

On July 12, 2016, Sauerbrunn was named to the 18-player team that would represent the United States at the 2016 Olympic Games in Rio de Janeiro.[226] She made her first appearance in Rio on August 3 in the team's opening match against New Zealand. The match marked her first Olympic start and she was subsequently named the Woman of the Match.[227] She also appeared in the second group match against France, helping the United States take away another victory and secure their first place finish in the group.[228] Sauerbrunn played all 90 minutes of the team's final group match against Colombia on August 9, which ended in a 2â2 draw.[229] In the quarterfinals, Sauerbrunn started in the match against Sweden on August 12. The game was tied 1â1 after both regulation time and extra time. The United States were then defeated by Sweden in penalty kicks.[230]
